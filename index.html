Offering Identity
What is it, who is it for
2
The Problem
Pain, prior attempts, urgency
Be specific. Name the symptom (not the solution). Quantify if you can — hours wasted, error rates, revenue impact.
This is gold for the pitch — it shows you understand why point solutions fail and positions Aberdeen's approach as different.
Could be market pressure, technology shift, regulatory change, competitor move, internal milestone. Helps Claude find supporting market data.
3
What They Get
Outcomes, deliverables, timeline
Write this from the client's perspective. What does their world look like after Aberdeen delivers? What can they do that they couldn't before?
Name each deliverable like an artifact. "Strategy sessions" is bad. "90-day AI roadmap with prioritized use case backlog" is good.
If your engagement has clear phases, list them here. This powers Slide 3's timeline layout. If it's more parallel work, skip this and the card layout will be used instead.
4
Why Aberdeen
Differentiation, stickiness, moat
This is your retention argument. Think about what would have to be true for a client to leave — and why that's unlikely.
5
The Ask
Pricing, metrics, next steps
At least one metric should be quantified. These go directly on Slide 5.
Name the specific steps and who owns each one. This becomes the 3 Next Steps on Slide 5.
These won't appear on the deck but will be flagged in Claude's summary so the team knows what to address in Q&A.
